About

My Journey

I began learning software development right after completing my O-Levels, practicing on office computers since I didn't own one. I always looked for jobs that gave me access to a computer - that was my gateway to learning.

In 2007, at age 21, I co-founded AtollTech with two housemates. We built websites, secured projects, and earned good money. Though the company eventually collapsed, it taught me valuable lessons about entrepreneurship and resilience.

Today, I run my own development company and founded WeLearn Institute, where I've taught over 100 students. Many of them are now working as professional developers - that's what I'm most proud of.

As a father of 9 children living on an island, I've mastered the art of working remotely while balancing family life. I'm passionate about teaching kids coding and mentoring the next generation of developers, both professionally and within my own community.
